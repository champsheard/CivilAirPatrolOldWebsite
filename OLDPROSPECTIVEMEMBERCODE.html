What is Civil Air Patrol?

Civil Air Patrol (CAP) is a private, nonprofit (501(c)(3)) organization and the official auxiliary of the United States Air Force. Founded on December 1, 1941, CAP originally supported the nation's defense through coastal and border patrol missions during World War II. Today, CAP is made up of civilian volunteers who serve their communities while wearing uniforms modeled after those of the U.S. Air Force.

Today, CAP carries out three congressionally chartered missions:

  • Emergency Services – Search and rescue, disaster relief, airborne photography, and support for federal, state, and local agencies.
  • Aerospace Education – Aviation and STEM education for youth and the public.
  • Cadet Programs – Leadership, character development, fitness, and aerospace education for young people.

CAP supports the U.S. Department of Homeland Security and many state, local, and nonprofit organizations through search and rescue, disaster response, and aerial imaging missions.

CAP is not a flying club. While CAP does not generally train adults to become pilots, qualified pilots can receive specialized training in search and rescue operations, mountain flying, crew resource management (CRM), and flight safety. Cadets may also qualify for flight training scholarships, including the Cadet Wings program.

Who Can Join as a Senior Member?

Adults age 18 and older who are U.S. citizens or permanent residents (green card holders) are eligible to join. Other legally admitted non-citizens may request a waiver of the citizenship requirement.

Our members come from all walks of life. While many have an interest in aviation, only about 20% are pilots. Members serve in a variety of roles, including:

  • Aircrew
  • Ground teams
  • Communications
  • Emergency services
  • Administration
  • Finance
  • Logistics
  • Education
  • Public Affairs

All adult members must complete a background screening through CAP National Headquarters, including submitting an FD-258 fingerprint card for an FBI criminal history check. Certain felony convictions or patterns of criminal activity may disqualify applicants. Fingerprinting is required for all adult members.

All Senior Members must also complete Cadet Protection Program Training (CPPT), regardless of whether they work directly with cadets.

Who Can Join as a Cadet?

Youth ages 12 through 18 may join the Cadet Program after attending three squadron meetings. Following your third visit, you'll complete the online membership application. Squadron leaders will guide you through the process and answer any questions.

To learn more about our Cadet Program, visit this page.

Do I Have to Be a Pilot?

No. Most CAP members are not pilots. There are many opportunities to serve in communications, emergency services, public affairs, administration, cadet mentoring, logistics, and incident command.

Non-pilots can also participate in flight missions as scanners, observers, airborne photographers, and operators of specialized mission equipment.

How Much Does It Cost to Join?

 

  • Senior Member National Dues: $65/year
  • Cadet National Dues: $38/year
  • Scottsdale Squadron Local Dues: $20 per calendar year

 

What Is Expected of Me?

CAP members are expected to uphold the organization's four Core Values:

  • Integrity
  • Volunteer Service
  • Excellence in All We Do
  • Respect

Members participate in ceremonies, including the Pledge of Allegiance, and wear approved CAP uniforms while taking part in official activities. Uniform options range from corporate polo attire to Air Force-style uniforms, depending on the activity.

New Senior Members are expected to complete:

  • Level I Orientation
  • Cadet Protection Program Training (CPPT)
  • Equal Opportunity (EO) Training

These requirements should be completed within six months of joining. Level I and CPPT are online courses followed by a discussion with the Squadron Commander or designee. Members become eligible to participate in missions and CAP flight activities after completing Level I.

Cadets are encouraged to advance regularly through the achievement program. Promotions provide leadership opportunities, scholarship eligibility, access to National Cadet Special Activities, and strengthen college and job applications. Earning the Mitchell Award (Cadet Second Lieutenant) is especially valuable for applicants to U.S. Service Academies.

All members are encouraged to serve in a squadron staff position. Running a successful squadron requires volunteers working together, and every contribution makes a difference.

What Will I Gain from CAP Membership?

The value of CAP membership depends on the effort you invest. Members gain the satisfaction of serving their community and country while building friendships with others who share an interest in aviation and public service.

CAP also offers opportunities to develop valuable leadership and technical skills, including:

  • Leadership and management
  • Search and rescue operations
  • Aircrew qualifications
  • Ground team operations
  • Radio communications
  • Incident Command Post operations
  • Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS/drone operations)
  • Professional development through U.S. Air Force Air University

If this sounds like the kind of organization where you'd like to serve your community, state, and country, please email us. We'd be happy to answer your questions and help you get started.

How to Join as a Senior Member

1. Visit the Squadron
Contact the Squadron Commander and attend at least two meetings.

2. Complete Your Application
Fill out the CAP Form 12 Membership Application and FD-258 Fingerprint Card.

Download the CAP Form 12 Membership Application

National dues are $65 annually, plus $20 annual Scottsdale Squadron dues.

3. Submit Your Paperwork
Make two copies of your completed application. Submit one copy to the squadron and keep one for your records.

4. Mail Your Original Documents

Civil Air Patrol / DP
105 S. Hansell St., Bldg. 714
Maxwell AFB, AL 36112

Note: FedEx and UPS deliver directly to CAP National Headquarters. USPS Priority Mail is routed through the Air Force Base mail system and may take longer to arrive.

5. Submit Payment
Payment may be made by check, money order, or credit card.

If paying by credit card, include:

  • Name on the card
  • Visa, MasterCard, or Discover card number
  • CVV security code
  • Expiration date

Once your application is received by CAP National Headquarters, it is processed in the order received.

You will receive an automated welcome email when your membership has been processed. A temporary membership card will be mailed to you while your background check is being completed.
